History

Traveling while studying is certainly very suitable for children. You can visit the Taman Pujaan Bangsa Margarana, in Bali. This tourist spot is always crowded on weekdays and holidays. Not only in the form of attractive beauty, but also the knowledge that you get

In addition to the heroes' grave gardens, there are also monuments or monuments that are silent witnesses to the heroic events of the puputan margarana. Behind it is a monument resembling a gravestone with the names of the heroes who died in the puputan margarana war led by I Gusti Ngurah Rai.

This monument was built in 1954, eight years after the heroic event of the puputan margarana on November 20, 1946. It was inaugurated by the central government as Taman Pahlawan Pujaan Bangsa and has an area of up to 25 hectares with a total of 1,342 tombstones for the heroes of the Indonesian independence war in Bali.

The gravestones are well kept clean. Among the gravestones, there is also a headstone dedicated to I Gusti Ngurah Rai, who was the leader of the troops in this heroic event.

Attraction

Built with the Tri Mandala concept, namely upstream, middle and downstream. Entering the park area, you will see an inscription containing the names of the heroes who were buried in Margarana Park. These heroes are national heroes under the leadership of I Gusti Ngurah Rai in the Margarana warfare.

Downstream, there is a ceremonial court, and two resting halls for visitors. There is also a statue of panca bakti depicting the unity and integrity of all the people in the struggle for independence.

In the middle, there is the Taman Pahlawan as high as 17 meters, with an 8-storey roof, and a 5 square foundation which symbolizes the proclamation of the Republic of Indonesia on August 17, 1945. On this monument also engraves the contents of I Gusti Ngurah Rai's reply to the Netherlands. The letter has the essence of the great spirit of struggle and patriotism of the Indonesian people who refuse to submit to NICA.

At the very end of the monument, there is the Historical Building. This building holds a number of relics of the struggle at that time. Among them were weapons that were looted from the NICA army, communication tools, disguises, medical devices and others.

Upstream, commonly known as Happy Park. In this section, there are thousands of small monuments which are symbols of the heroes who died in the battle of puputan. It also includes a headstone for an unknown hero.

Location
